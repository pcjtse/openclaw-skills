## 1. Executive Summary
NVIDIA (NVDA) is the undisputed powerhouse of the global semiconductor industry, particularly within the realm of artificial intelligence (AI) accelerators where it maintains a staggering 70-95% market share. Despite trading at a massive valuation premium, Wall Street sentiment remains near-euphoric due to continuous, expectation-shattering revenue growth in its Data Center business. The primary threats are intensifying competitive efforts from rivals (AMD, Broadcom) and major cloud customers developing custom in-house silicon.

## 3. Tier 2: Fundamental Analysis (FactSet/Refinitiv Equivalent)
*Insights drawn from the latest official 10-K and 10-Q filings from SEC EDGAR.*

*   **Business Drivers & Revenue:** The Data Center segment is the absolute core driver, significantly outpacing the legacy Gaming business. The rapid adoption of Hopper (H100) and upcoming Blackwell architectures are the primary revenue engines.
*   **Risk Factors highlighted in SEC Filings:**
    *   **Customer Concentration:** A massive portion of Nvidia's revenue comes from a handful of hyper-scalers (Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, Google).
    *   **Supply Chain Dependencies:** Heavy reliance on TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company) for fabrication. Geopolitical tensions in Taiwan present a critical, unhedgeable structural risk.
    *   **Export Restrictions:** U.S. government regulations restricting the sale of advanced AI chips to China and other specified regions continue to impact total addressable market (TAM) potential.
    *   **Intense Competition:** Rapidly evolving competition attempting to break Nvidia's CUDA software moat.
